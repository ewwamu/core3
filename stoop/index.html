Of these unconventional "chairs", I find that stoops are where people tend to congregate the most. It is easy to walk by a stoop full of people conversing or eating, having a quiet moment to themselves. There is a profound sense of community and comradery between neighbors. However, there are many who don't share the same sentiment.

Of course, those who live in the building of the stoop have a hard time welcoming the large groups who take up all the space surrounding the entrance, and many of these sitters are comfortable with leaving their trash and cigarette butts upon the steps. Many have taken to installing a fence, or putting up a "Private Property" sign in hopes of keeping these hordes away, but New Yorkers, ever a cheeky and rule-breaking bunch, keep coming back.
