Despite New York City being one of the most population dense cities of America, places to leisurely rest and sit and few and far between. Why is that? I think Please, Just a Nice Place to Sit, an article from the New York Times, provides the reason, or at least one, plain and clear. The quote is as follows:

"But there are still few places to sit — and not entirely by accident, either. Some builders genuinely and sincerely mistrust people — noncustomer people, at least—and are almost obsessive on the threat of hippies."

There is some outdated language being used(I mean, "hippies"?), but points out how NYC's infrastructure is not made to support leisurely rest, thus why this unconventional collection came into being.

Fire hydrants are byfar one of the most popular places to sit, no matter how uncomfortable it may be. I saw many resting for a smoke, to pause inbetween destinations, and even to take a short lunch break. Its both an impressive and humorous representation of New York City's personality and community, as well as a byproduct of a larger issue on city planning and design that needs to be addressed.
