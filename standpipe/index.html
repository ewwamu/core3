Standpipes are right up there with hydrants in terms of popularity and accessibility. In fact, many would say they prefer the standpipe, as its wide top provides ample surface area for one's tush. At the perfect sitting height and high frequency along the sidewalks, I am happy to agree with the many. Thus, it is equally as unfortunate that I must report: these "chairs" are also being taken away.

On streets and sidewalks with busy foot traffic, you may notice that these seats are now covered by threatening and morose cages of spikes. Though their official purpose it to keep the city's pests away, it's easy to see the true, more sinister reason; that is, to be as uninviting and inaccessible as possible. We slowly find ourselves with less and less spaces to occupy in nonconsumer ways, to meet and connect and take breaks, and its disheartening. However, I also believe in the unconventional and wonderful places New Yorkers will find to rest.
